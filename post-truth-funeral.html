Post-truth Funeral

I dreamt of Paulie Gualtieri

Halted in place, yet moving with forbearing

Conveyor belt at his feet

Moving him slowly

Towards the coffin, grief accreting

Deacons speaking:

Lord, most holy.

He wore a mourning shawl,

Dark-veiled & effeminate.

Others balled up laughing at him

Too terrified to be affectionate

Something to drawl on between sobs

To their therapists, feeling delicate

Paulie threw gold coins to the floor in sorrow and waring

His conveyor belt slowly moved him towards the other mourners

Namely, four old dears, ornate-adorned just for the coroner.

No gold enough to save those who’ve pushed sin to it’s limit

Departed’s name drawn in a foriegn tongue on the floor in spirits

Odd shapes the alcohol made were as if that of Latin digits

There was a whiff about them

These almost vermouth numerals

Stiff, black-out yielding gems they were

At the post-truth funeral.
